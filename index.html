Our Heritage

A Hotel Born
From Cinema

Named after the Georgian word for "poster," Hotel Afisha celebrates the golden age of Georgian filmmaking. Each floor is an homage to a legendary director. Each room, a scene waiting to unfold. From Sergei Parajanov's visual poetry to Otar Iosseliani's gentle wit — the spirit of Georgian cinema lives in every frame of your stay.

Tbilisi
Philharmonic

Steps from Hotel Afisha stands the iconic Tbilisi Concert Hall — a striking Soviet modernist landmark built in 1971 by architect Ivane Chkhenkeli. Home to the Georgian Philharmonic Orchestra since 1925, this 2,300-seat venue remains the cultural heartbeat of the city.
